About Me

Bridging the gap between traditional legal frameworks and the digital frontier.

My Journey

I am a dedicated law student with a profound interest in how technology reshapes society and the legal systems governing it. My academic journey is driven by a simple question: How do we build regulatory frameworks that foster innovation while protecting fundamental rights?

Whether it's the decentralized nature of blockchain, the ethical implications of AI, or the pressing need for robust data privacy laws, I believe the legal profession must proactively engage with technologists. My goal is to become a leading voice at this intersection, providing credible, analytical insights into the legal challenges of our time.

What I Focus On

Data Privacy & Regulatory Compliance

As data becomes the new global currency, protecting it is paramount. I study the implications of frameworks like the NDPA and GDPR, helping organizations understand their obligations and the legal rights of individuals in the digital age.

Cybercrime

The borderless nature of the internet presents unique jurisdictional and prosecutorial challenges. My focus lies in analyzing regional and international legal instruments aimed at combating cyber warfare, fraud, and digital exploitation.

Blockchain

Decentralization challenges traditional notions of liability, property, and contract law. I explore the legal status of cryptocurrencies, smart contracts, and the regulatory approaches required to safely integrate blockchain into the mainstream economy.

AI Governance

Artificial Intelligence poses existential and ethical questions. I analyze emerging global policies, such as the EU AI Act, to understand how liability, intellectual property, and legal personhood apply to autonomous systems.
